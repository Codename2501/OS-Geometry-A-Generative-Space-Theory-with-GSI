# MatsuiOS: OS Geometry & GSI (Generative Space Integral)

Welcome to the official repository for **OS Geometry**, the core mathematical framework of **MatsuiOS**.

Traditional mathematics and physics rely on a fundamental assumption: the existence of a static, pre-defined space and a fixed differential ($dx$). **OS Geometry** shatters this assumption. It is a generative theory where space, differentials, derivatives, and integrals are not pre-existing conditions, but are dynamically **generated** by an Operating System (MatsuiOS).

---

## 🌟 Core Philosophy: The Mathematics of Generation

In OS Geometry, the universe is built step-by-step through the **Generative Function $G$**:

$$
G(x,S) = (\vec d,\ \ell,\ S')
$$

Instead of measuring an already existing space, the OS autonomously determines the "next step" by generating its own direction ($\vec d_{\text{OS}}$) and scale ($\ell_{\text{OS}}$). This creates a trajectory, bringing the computational space into existence only as it is needed.

---

## 📐 Key Architectures

### 1. The Generative Differential ($d_{\text{OS}}x$)

Forget the classical $dx$. The OS generates its own differential based on real-time internal state evaluation:

$$
d_{\text{OS}}x = \ell_{\text{OS}}(x)\,\vec d_{\text{OS}}(x)
$$

### 2. MDAD (OS-Derivative)

**MatsuiOS Directed Adaptive Derivative** is the difference measured strictly along the OS-generated trajectory. Instead of a theoretical limit in a fixed direction, MDAD is a tangible result of the generation process:

$$
\text{MDAD}_f(x_k) = f(x_{k+1}) - f(x_k)
$$

### 3. GSI (Generative Space Integral)

The **Generative Space Integral** measures the generated world. Unlike ordinary integrals that sum over a fixed domain, GSI accumulates an observation function $f(x)$ along the trajectory dynamically generated by the OS:

$$
\int_{\text{GSI}} f(x)\, d_{\text{OS}}x \approx \sum_k f(x_k)\,\ell_{\text{OS}}(x_k)
$$

By changing the "lens" $f(x)$, we can evaluate the stability, energy, and behavior of the generated system.
